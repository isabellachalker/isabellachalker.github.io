Illustrating Louis Tomlinson: An Illustration Book

This project is an illustration book inspired by Louis Tomlinson’s discography that I spent a semester in college working on. I digitally illustrated a piece for each song in his discography and included lyrics that inspired the illustration alongside them. I also included commentary on the creative process behind the illustrations, as well as the meaning behind each song and my interpretation of the lyrics.

The book is organized chronologically, starting with his debut singles and continuing through both of his albums. Each illustration reflects the themes and emotional tone of its corresponding song. I formatted, printed, and hand-bound two copies as hardcover books. This project not only celebrates Louis Tomlinson’s music but also serves as a personal exploration of how music inspires visual art.

Independent Study

Dust Jacket Spread

Creating this book required extensive research, planning, and skill development in illustration, layout design, and bookbinding techniques. The final product is 88 pages.

This project was important because it integrated multiple creative disciplines into one cohesive project. It allowed me to create a coherent body of work that demonstrates my ability to interpret music through visual storytelling. By undertaking this project, I aimed to develop my technical skills, improve my ability to manage a large-scale creative project, and produce a professional-grade book.
